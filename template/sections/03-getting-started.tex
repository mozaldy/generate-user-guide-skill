\section{Getting Started}

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{System Requirements}

Before accessing Insight Doc, ensure your environment meets the
following minimum requirements:

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{} l X @{}}
  \toprule
  \textbf{Requirement} & \textbf{Specification} \\
  \midrule
  Web Browser
    & Google Chrome 120+, Mozilla Firefox 115+,
      or Microsoft Edge 120+ \\
  Internet Connection
    & Minimum 5~Mbps (recommended 10~Mbps for
      large document preview and download) \\
  Screen Resolution
    & Minimum 1280\,\texttimes\,720; optimized for
      1920\,\texttimes\,1080 and above \\
  JavaScript
    & Must be enabled in your browser \\
  Cookies
    & Must be enabled for session management \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\begin{ugNote}
  Safari 17+ is partially supported. Some advanced features
  (such as real-time WebSocket notifications) may have limited
  functionality on Safari.
\end{ugNote}

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Accessing the Application}

\begin{ugSteps}
  \item Open your web browser and navigate to the Insight Doc URL
        provided by your organization
        (e.g., \texttt{https://insight.company.com}).
  \item You will be redirected to the \textbf{Keycloak SSO}
        login page.
  \item Enter your \ugField{Username} (or \ugField{Corporate Email})
        and \ugField{Password}.
  \item Click \ugButton{Sign In}.
  \item If Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is enabled on your
        account, enter the verification code from your
        authenticator app.
  \item Upon successful authentication, you will be redirected
        to the Insight Doc \textbf{Dashboard} appropriate for
        your role.
\end{ugSteps}

\begin{ugTip}
  Bookmark the application URL for quick access. Since Insight Doc
  uses Keycloak Single Sign-On (SSO), you may be automatically
  logged in if you already have an active SSO session from another
  corporate application.
\end{ugTip}

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{First Login}

On your first login, Keycloak may prompt you to:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=18pt]
  \item \textbf{Update your password} --- Set a strong password
        that meets your organization's security policy.
  \item \textbf{Configure MFA} --- Scan the QR code with an
        authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft
        Authenticator, or similar).
  \item \textbf{Accept Terms of Use} --- Review and accept the
        organization's terms and conditions.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{ugWarning}
  If you cannot log in or did not receive credentials, contact the
  IT Help Desk at \texttt{helpdesk@sindika.co.id} or extension
  \textbf{4500}. Account provisioning is managed centrally through
  Keycloak by your system administrator.
\end{ugWarning}

\subsection{Understanding Your Role}

After login, your experience is shaped by your assigned role.
The following table summarizes what each role can do:

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{} l l X @{}}
  \toprule
  \textbf{Role} & \textbf{Badge}
    & \textbf{What You Can Do} \\
  \midrule
  Admin
    & \ugRole{Admin}
    & Full system access --- upload, review, approve,
      manage all documents, users, master data,
      access control, and system configuration \\
  Manager
    & \ugRole{Manager}
    & Manage documents and approvals within your unit;
      grant/revoke access; create share links;
      view admin-level dashboards \\
  Reviewer
    & \ugRole{Reviewer}
    & Review submitted documents; approve or reject
      with comments; view the review pipeline
      dashboard \\
  Staff
    & \ugRole{Staff}
    & Upload and manage your own documents; request
      access to restricted documents; view personal
      dashboard and download documents \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\begin{ugNote}
  Your role determines both the menu items visible in the
  sidebar and the actions available on each page. If you
  believe your role is incorrect, contact your administrator.
\end{ugNote}

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Dashboard Overview After Login}

Upon login, you will see a personalized dashboard based on
your role:

\begin{description}[leftmargin=18pt, style=nextline]
  \item[\ugRole{Admin} / \ugRole{Manager} Dashboard]
    Full organizational overview including total documents,
    storage usage, download trends (30-day chart), distribution
    by status/type/unit, top~10 most-downloaded documents,
    pending review and access request counts, and a live
    activity feed.

  \item[\ugRole{Reviewer} Dashboard]
    Review pipeline focus --- pending review count, approved and
    rejected counts for the current month, status pipeline
    visualization, documents awaiting review (sorted by oldest
    first), and recent status change history.

  \item[\ugRole{Staff} Dashboard]
    Personal document overview --- your total documents, those
    in review, rejected items, a status breakdown donut chart,
    draft documents with quick-action buttons, pending access
    requests, and your recent activity feed.
\end{description}

\subsection{Changing Your Language}

Insight Doc supports two languages:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=18pt]
  \item \textbf{English} (default)
  \item \textbf{Bahasa Indonesia}
\end{itemize}

To switch languages:

\begin{ugSteps}
  \item Click your \textbf{profile avatar} in the top-right
        corner of the header.
  \item Select \ugMenu{Language} from the dropdown menu.
  \item Choose your preferred language.
  \item The interface will reload in the selected language.
\end{ugSteps}

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Logging Out \& Session Management}

\subsubsection*{Logging Out}
\begin{ugSteps}
  \item Click your \textbf{profile avatar} in the top-right
        corner.
  \item Select \ugMenu{Logout} from the dropdown.
  \item You will be redirected to the Keycloak logout page,
        which also terminates your SSO session across all
        connected applications.
\end{ugSteps}

\subsubsection*{Session Timeout}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=18pt]
  \item Your session is managed by Keycloak via JWT tokens
        with a configurable expiry (typically 30~minutes for
        access tokens, 8~hours for refresh tokens).
  \item If your session expires while you are working, the
        application will prompt you to re-authenticate.
  \item Active sessions are automatically extended when you
        interact with the application.
\end{itemize}

\begin{ugWarning}
  Always log out when using shared or public computers. Closing
  the browser tab alone does \textbf{not} terminate your SSO
  session --- use the \ugMenu{Logout} option to fully sign out.
\end{ugWarning}

\subsection{Navigating for the First Time}

After login, here is a recommended first-time exploration
sequence:

\begin{ugSteps}
  \item \textbf{Explore the Dashboard} --- Your landing page
        shows role-specific analytics and pending action items.
        Familiarize yourself with the stat cards and activity
        timeline.
  \item \textbf{Browse Documents} --- Click \ugMenu{Documents}
        in the sidebar to see the document list. Try switching
        between list view and thumbnail grid view.
  \item \textbf{Try a Search} --- Use the search bar in the
        header to search for a known document by title or
        keyword.
  \item \textbf{Open a Document} --- Click any document to view
        its detail page. Explore the tabs: metadata, version
        history, timeline, access, and share.
  \item \textbf{Preview a Document} --- Click \ugButton{Preview}
        to view a document in your browser without downloading.
  \item \textbf{Check Your Profile} --- Click your avatar to
        verify your name, role, and language preference.
\end{ugSteps}

\begin{ugTip}
  If you are a \ugRole{Staff} user, start by uploading a test
  document to experience the full workflow: upload, submit for
  review, and track status changes on your dashboard.
\end{ugTip}

\subsection{Browser Configuration Tips}

For the best experience with Insight Doc:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=18pt]
  \item \textbf{Enable pop-ups} for the application domain ---
        some download and share link operations may open new
        tabs.
  \item \textbf{Allow cookies} --- required for session
        management and SSO authentication.
  \item \textbf{Enable JavaScript} --- the application requires
        JavaScript for all interactive features.
  \item \textbf{Disable PDF-blocking extensions} --- browser
        extensions that intercept PDF files may interfere with
        the in-browser preview feature.
  \item \textbf{Use zoom at 100\%} --- the interface is designed
        for standard zoom levels; extreme zoom may affect
        layout rendering.
\end{itemize}
